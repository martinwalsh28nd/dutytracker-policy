# DutyTracker — Quick Start 🏥⏱️

It logs your hospital duty hours for you in the background, then files them to
MedHub with one tap on Sunday. Built by a co-resident because I was tired of
backfilling MedHub. Thanks for kicking the tires.

## Setup — about 2 minutes

1. Tap the TestFlight link, install, and open it.
2. Onboarding: pick your program (**General Surgery** or **EM**), enter your
   **last name** (so it can spot you on the call schedule), and — the important
   one — allow location **"Always."** That's what lets it log your hours in the
   background. Say yes to notifications too.
3. *Optional:* connect **MedHub** with your normal login for one-tap
   submitting. Your password stays on your phone and is only ever sent to
   MedHub — never to me or any server.

## How it works

- All week it quietly notes when you **arrive at and leave the hospital**. You
  do nothing.
- **Every Sunday** it nudges you at **9 AM, 10 AM, and noon**: open the app, tap
  **Log Now**, glance at the week, hit **Submit**. About 30 seconds, done.
- **What you're submitting:** the week that *just ended* — last Sunday through
  yesterday (Saturday). You're always one week behind, which is exactly what
  MedHub wants. (Today doesn't count yet; it goes in next Sunday.)

## First time? A few things to make it smooth

- Your first **Log Now** pre-fills a **Mon–Fri, 5a–5p** placeholder week so
  you're *editing*, not staring at a blank screen. **Tap each day to fix the
  times to what you actually worked,** uncheck any day off, and use **"Add a
  day"** for weekend shifts. Then submit.
- The single most important setting is location **"Always."** If it's set to
  "While Using," it can't auto-log — double-check in **iPhone Settings →
  DutyTracker** if tracking ever looks off.
- It **never submits anything on its own.** You always review and tap Submit.
